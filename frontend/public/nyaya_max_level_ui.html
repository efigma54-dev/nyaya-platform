You are being arrested — your rights right now
DEMAND to know why you are being arrested (Article 22 — Constitution)
You have the RIGHT to call a lawyer immediately
Police MUST produce you before a magistrate within 24 hours
Do NOT sign anything without a lawyer present
Police arrested me without telling me the reason. What are my rights?
Direct answer: When arrested, you have the constitutional right to be told the grounds of arrest immediately (Article 22), the right to a lawyer, and the right to be produced before a magistrate within 24 hours.

Applicable laws:
— Article 22 (Constitution): Right against arbitrary arrest. Police must inform you of the reason for arrest. You may not be detained beyond 24 hours without magistrate authority.
— Article 21 (Constitution): No person shall be deprived of life or personal liberty except by procedure established by law.

What you should do:
1. Calmly ask the arresting officer: "What are the grounds of my arrest?"
2. Immediately request to call your lawyer or a family member.
3. Remember or write down the arresting officer's name and badge number.
4. Do not sign any document before consulting a lawyer.

Important: If police refuse to state the reason or deny access to a lawyer, that is a constitutional violation. File a complaint with the State Human Rights Commission or approach the High Court under Article 226.

Constitution of India — Fundamental Rights
Protection against arrest and detention
When arrested: must be told WHY immediately. Right to a lawyer. Must be produced before magistrate within 24 hours. Police cannot detain longer without magistrate authority.
Magistrate Court / High Court
Bare act text
No person who is arrested shall be detained in custody without being informed, as soon as may be, of the grounds for such arrest nor shall he be denied the right to consult, and to be defended by, a legal practitioner of his choice. Every person who is arrested and detained in custody shall be produced before the nearest magistrate within a period of twenty-four hours of such arrest...
§ 21Constitutional right
Constitution of India — Fundamental Rights
Protection of life and personal liberty
Nobody can be arrested or detained except by fair legal process. This is the most fundamental right — it underlies every other liberty.
Supreme Court (Article 32)
Bare act text
No person shall be deprived of his life or personal liberty except according to procedure established by law.
§ 32Constitutional right
Constitution of India — Fundamental Rights
Right to constitutional remedies
If any fundamental right is violated, you can directly approach the Supreme Court. This is itself a fundamental right — Dr. Ambedkar called it the "heart and soul" of the Constitution.

The right to move the Supreme Court by appropriate proceedings for the enforcement of the rights conferred by this Part is guaranteed.
